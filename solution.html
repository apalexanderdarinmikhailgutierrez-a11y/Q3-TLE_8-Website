Livelihood Awareness

In order to give recognition to FCS, we've displayed a campaign awareness plan.

Behind The Campaign

The solution directly addresses this by organizing a formal meeting or information campaign to educate both students and teachers about the real-life importance and academic value of TLE. By explaining how TLE helps develop practical life skills, improves grades, and prepares students for future careers and adulthood, we can change their mindset and increase interest in the subject.

How it's Done

Organizing a meeting or campaign can be done within school hours using available classrooms, audiovisual equipment, and teacher support. Posters, short presentations, and simple demonstrations can be prepared by students and teachers without needing a large budget. Asking for additional funding from the government or school administration can be done gradually while awareness activities start immediately.

Who are involved?

The project will be carried out by both students and professors. Students will assist in creating posters, presentations, and brief discussions regarding the significance of TLE. Teachers will moderate the conversation, authorise materials, and help plan the meeting or campaign.

Challenges

  • Limited budget for materials
  • Lack of interest or participation from some students
  • Scheduling conflicts with other school activities
  • Delays in receiving additional funding
  • Limited time for preparation

These can be addressed by using low-cost materials, encouraging teamwork, and coordinating early with teachers and administrators.
